Code time!

follow these steps:

  1. Download Firmware.zip
  2. Extract all files to new folder ("Firmware")
  3. Open "firmware.ino" using Arduino IDE (You can download it here)
  4. Make sure the board is connected to your computer via USB
  5. In Arduino IDE- don't forget to select the correct Port and Board in the tool menu.
  6. Upload "Firmware.ino" to your board.

    7. Great! now you can move on to the Build section.

The firmware.zip contains all the libraries and sample code that will help you understand how to read values from your sensors and operate your actuators through the controller.

It's a sample which you'll need to adjust to your needs.

IoT Service

The code enables your input devices to post messages to a message store on Dweet.io, and your outputs to be controlled remotely. Once your Freeboard is created, messages sent by your device to Dweet.io will also be sent to your Freeboard.

See how the integration into our IoT Service works by reviewing the code sample we provided - you can play and modify the code sample. Note that any change you make in how you post to Dweet.io may have an effect on the Freeboard, and may cause the IoT Service to improperly function.

Data which you or your device "dweets" will be publicly viewable on dweet.io as well as on Freeboard, and may be edited by anyone, unless and until you purchase a private plan from Dweet and apply the plan to your device's feeds on Dweet and Freeboard. Even if you create private plans for both Dweet and Freeboard, data previously sent may remain publicly available.

Prior to using Dweet or Freeboard, we recommend taking a look at their respective websites, plans, features, terms of use and privacy policies all of which can be found at their websites at Dweet.io and Freeboard.io (Freeboard does not post terms of use or a privacy policy).
